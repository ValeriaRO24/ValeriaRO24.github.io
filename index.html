Welcome to my academic and professional space! I am currently an undergraduate at the University of Toronto, pursuing a Specialist in Computer Science with a Mathematics Minor. I am soon to complete my semester abroad at the University of Granada in Spain. Then I am on track to continue my academic journey at the University of McMaster with a Masters in Engineering.

In addition to English, I speak Spanish fluently and have intermediate proficiency in French, which allows me to engage with diverse communities and perspectives.

I'm excited to share my academic journey, projects, and passions with you through this space. Thanks for stopping by—feel free to explore and connect!

Research Interests

    Throughout my academic journey, I've developed a strong interest in the field of Human-Computer Interaction, with a particular focus on how artificial intelligence can support and enhance human life—especially when applied thoughtfully and responsibly. I'm especially drawn to the intersection of AI and computer science education, and how emerging technologies are reshaping the way we teach, learn, and engage with digital tools. My research also explores the psychological dimensions of educational technology—how students and educators interact with intelligent systems, and how those experiences can be improved. In addition to these core interests, I'm passionate about supporting this work through robust software development practices and the effective use of tools like databases and educational platforms.
